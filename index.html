The Eduba frame

Most AI initiatives get automated at the wrong layer. The consulting value is not the sprint itself. It is the decision about which layer each piece of the client's workflow belongs on, captured in a form that any downstream partner can build against without re-interviewing the client.

60%

Traditional

Code and database work. Postgres, ETL, plain services. Faster, cheaper, with better guarantees than an LLM.

30%

Rule-based

Workflow engines and document parsers. Deterministic logic where the audit trail has to hold.

10%

Genuine AI

Where an LLM or a bespoke model actually earns its keep. Small surface, high leverage, scoped carefully.

About 60% of the problems a Sapient sprint surfaces are traditional code and database work. About 30% are rule-based logic that belongs in a workflow engine or a document parser. About 10% are genuine AI problems where an LLM or a bespoke model actually earns its keep.

The relevant case study

KPMG UK, one of the Big Four

Forty-plus executives ran through a regulated-industry methodology engagement with Jake. Senior audience. Consulting-firm to consulting-firm delivery. No vendor pitch in the room, just methodology that mapped to the specific governance constraints KPMG's clients live with.

That is the engagement shape that applies here: a peer-to-peer methodology transfer that Sapient can incorporate into its own sprint deliverable, rather than a vendor displacing the relationship.

The relevant paper

Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM)

Submitted to ACM TiiS and open-sourced under MIT, ICM specifies agent context as a layered filesystem with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains, which is exactly the spec format a sprint output needs to survive the hand-off to a build partner.
